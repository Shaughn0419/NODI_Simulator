## 0. 一条论文主线 / One Paper-Only Main Chain

只看 Tsuyama 论文中的散射与干涉增强主链，可以压缩成：

$$
\text{blank-channel phase delay}
\to
\text{channel diffraction}
\to
E_D
\to
E_S
\to
I_t = |E_D + E_S|^2
\to
\text{pulse-like detectable signal}.
$$

这里：

- $E_D$：空白通道在 diffracted-light region 中提供的参考场 / reference field provided by the blank channel in the diffracted-light region
- $E_S$：颗粒散射场 / particle scattering field
- $I_t$：探测器记录的总光强 / total detected intensity

特别重要的参数 / Especially important parameters：

- $\lambda$：探测光波长 / probe wavelength。它同时进入相位延迟、Mie 尺寸参数和角向散射响应，是最重要的尺度参数之一。
- $n_s-n_g$：溶剂与玻璃的折射率差 / refractive-index mismatch between solvent and glass。它决定空白通道相位滤波强度，从而决定参考场形成方式。
- $d$：通道深度 / channel depth。它与折射率差一起决定光程差和相位延迟。
- $a$：颗粒半径 / particle radius。它决定散射强度标度，是区分小颗粒与大颗粒响应的关键参数。
- $\phi$：参考场和散射场之间的相对相位 / relative phase between the reference field and the scattering field。它决定干涉项是增强还是抵消。

记号提醒 / Notation reminder：

- 本文中的 $\theta$ 在第一部分始终指“相位延迟” / in Section 1, $\theta$ always means the phase delay.
- 到 Mie 散射部分，为避免混淆，散射角单独记作 $\theta_s$ / in the Mie-scattering part, the scattering angle is written separately as $\theta_s$.

## 1. 从相位延迟到参考场 / From Phase Delay to the Reference Field

### 1.1 相位延迟的起点 / Starting from the Phase Delay

Tsuyama 2020 diffraction 论文的起点是：通道内部与玻璃区域的折射率不同，因此会产生光程差，对应相位延迟

$$
\theta = \frac{2\pi (n_s-n_g)d}{\lambda}.
$$

参数说明 / Parameter meaning：

- $\theta$：通道引入的相位延迟 / phase delay introduced by the channel
- $n_s$：通道内溶剂或介质折射率 / refractive index of the solvent or medium inside the channel
- $n_g$：玻璃基底或通道壁折射率 / refractive index of the glass substrate or wall
- $d$：通道深度 / channel depth
- $\lambda$：探测光波长 / probe wavelength

重要参数补充 / Why the key parameters matter：

- $n_s-n_g$ 越大，相位滤波越强，通道越不再像“几乎透明”的弱扰动对象。
- $d$ 越大，光程差越大，但在论文口径里，它首先是通过相位延迟进入，而不是直接当作独立的振幅增益。
- $\lambda$ 越短，同样的折射率差和深度会产生更大的相位延迟。

### 1.2 通道作为相位滤波器 / The Channel as a Phase Filter

在论文语义下，通道可以看成横向上的相位窗。若通道宽度为 $W$，中心位置为 $x_c$，则横向透射函数可写成

$$
t(x)
=
1+\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)\Pi\!\left(\frac{x-x_c}{W}\right),
$$

其中：

- $t(x)$：横向透射函数 / transverse transmission function
- $\Pi(\cdot)$：矩形窗函数 / rectangular window function
- $W$：通道宽度 / channel width
- $x_c$：通道中心位置 / channel-center position

它的物理意义是：

- 通道外：透射因子为 $1$，即不额外改变相位
- 通道内：透射因子为 $e^{i\theta}$，即附加相位延迟

### 1.3 通道后场如何被分解 / How the Post-Channel Field Is Decomposed

若入射场为 $E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)$，则通道后场为

$$
E_{\mathrm{after}}(x)
=
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)\,t(x)
=
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)
+
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)\Pi\!\left(\frac{x-x_c}{W}\right).
$$

这一步非常关键，因为它把总场拆成了两部分：

1. $E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)$：未受通道相位扰动的主透射项 / undisturbed transmitted component
2. $E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)(e^{i\theta}-1)\Pi(\cdot)$：由通道局域相位差产生的扰动项 / perturbation term generated by the local phase contrast of the channel

因此，参考场并不是“凭空出现”的，而是通道相位扰动在传播后形成的衍射分量。

### 1.4 传播到后焦面：为什么会出现衍射参考场 / Propagation to the Back Focal Plane: Why a Diffracted Reference Field Appears

传播到后焦面后，电场变成横向场的傅里叶变换：

$$
\tilde E(k_x)
=
\mathcal{F}\!\left\{E_{\mathrm{after}}(x)\right\}
=
\tilde E_{\mathrm{in}}(k_x)
+
\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)
\mathcal{F}\!\left\{
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)\Pi\!\left(\frac{x-x_c}{W}\right)
\right\}.
$$

参数说明 / Parameter meaning：

- $\tilde E(k_x)$：后焦面或角谱平面中的复场 / complex field in the back focal plane or angular-spectrum plane
- $k_x$：横向空间频率，等价于一个角谱坐标 / transverse spatial frequency, equivalent to an angular-spectrum coordinate
- $\tilde E_{\mathrm{in}}(k_x)$：未扰动主透射束的角谱 / angular spectrum of the undisturbed transmitted beam

这里最核心的一点是：

$$
\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)
$$

这个因子直接决定了由通道相位扰动产生的衍射分量有多强。也就是说，**参考场的种子来自相位差，而不是来自颗粒**。

### 1.5 弱相位极限下的直观解释 / Intuition in the Weak-Phase Limit

当相位延迟较小时，

$$
e^{i\theta}-1
\approx
i\theta - \frac{\theta^2}{2},
\qquad
|e^{i\theta}-1|
=
2\left|\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\right|
\approx
|\theta|.
$$

这表示在弱相位延迟条件下：

- 衍射参考场幅值近似与 $|\theta|$ 成正比
- 因而近似与

$$
\left|\frac{(n_s-n_g)d}{\lambda}\right|
$$

成正比

### 1.6 从衍射角谱到探测端参考场 / From the Diffracted Angular Spectrum to the Detector-Side Reference Field

论文实验并不是收集全部角谱，而是只收集 diffracted-light region。于是探测端参考场可以抽象写成

$$
E_D
\propto
\int_{\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{diff}}}
\tilde E_{\mathrm{diff}}(k_x,k_z)\,dA,
$$

其中：

- $E_D$：论文中用于干涉的通道衍射参考场 / channel-diffracted reference field used for interference
- $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{diff}}$：被探测器、狭缝、针孔和物镜 NA 选中的 diffracted-light region / diffracted-light region selected by the detector, slit, pinhole, and objective NA
- $\tilde E_{\mathrm{diff}}$：由相位扰动产生的衍射角谱分量 / diffracted angular-spectrum component generated by the phase perturbation
- $dA$：角谱平面或后焦面上的积分面积元 / area element in the angular-spectrum or back-focal plane

这里还需要强调一点 / One more crucial point here：

- transmitted-light region 不是作为参考场直接拿来用 / the transmitted-light region is not used directly as the reference field
- 论文强调的是：主透射区域被空间筛选抑制，而 diffracted-light region 被保留下来 / the papers emphasize that the transmitted-light region is spatially suppressed, while the diffracted-light region is retained

因此，$E_D$ 不是“整个透射束”，而是**被空间选择后的通道衍射分量**。

也就是说，完整逻辑是：

$$
\theta
\to
\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)
\to
\text{phase-perturbation term}
\to
\tilde E_{\mathrm{diff}}
\to
\text{diffracted-light-region selection}
\to
E_D.
$$

这就是 Tsuyama 论文中“空白通道先提供参考场”的真正物理含义。

## 2. 颗粒散射场如何进入问题 / How the Particle Scattering Field Enters the Problem

### 2.1 Mie 理论中的基本参数 / Basic Parameters in Mie Theory

对球形颗粒，先定义

$$
x=\frac{2\pi n_m a}{\lambda},
\qquad
m=\frac{\tilde n_p}{n_m},
\qquad
k_m=\frac{2\pi n_m}{\lambda}.
$$

参数说明 / Parameter meaning：

- $x$：尺寸参数 / size parameter
- $n_m$：周围介质折射率 / refractive index of the surrounding medium
- $a$：颗粒半径 / particle radius
- $\lambda$：探测波长 / probe wavelength
- $m$：颗粒相对复折射率 / relative complex refractive index of the particle
- $\tilde n_p$：颗粒复折射率 / complex refractive index of the particle
- $k_m$：介质中的波数 / wavenumber in the medium

重要参数补充 / Why the key parameters matter：

- $x$ 决定颗粒处于 Rayleigh 区还是更完整的 Mie 区，因此直接影响粒径标度。
- $\tilde n_p$ 的实部影响散射强度和相位，虚部与吸收损耗有关。
- $n_m$ 一旦变化，不仅尺寸参数会变，角向散射分布也会变。

### 2.2 散射强度与角向分布 / Scattering Strength and Angular Distribution

Mie 理论给出总散射效率

$$
Q_{\mathrm{sca}}
=
\frac{2}{x^2}
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}
(2n+1)\left(|a_n|^2+|b_n|^2\right),
$$

以及角向散射振幅函数

$$
S_1(\theta_s),\qquad S_2(\theta_s),
$$

从而得到微分散射截面

$$
\frac{dC_{\mathrm{sca}}}{d\Omega}
=
\frac{|S_1|^2+|S_2|^2}{2k_m^2}.
$$

参数说明 / Parameter meaning：

- $Q_{\mathrm{sca}}$：无量纲散射效率 / dimensionless scattering efficiency
- $a_n,b_n$：Mie 系数 / Mie coefficients
- $S_1,S_2$：角向散射振幅函数 / angular scattering amplitude functions
- $C_{\mathrm{sca}}$：散射截面 / scattering cross section
- $\Omega$：立体角 / solid angle
- $\theta_s$：散射角 / scattering angle

### 2.3 为什么论文里要从纯散射转到场幅 / Why the Paper Moves from Pure Scattering to Field Amplitude

纯散射强度满足

$$
I_{\mathrm{sca}} \propto |E_S|^2.
$$

因此若已知一个强度量，例如纯散射或微分散射截面，则进入干涉问题时要转到场幅量：

$$
E_S \propto \sqrt{I_{\mathrm{sca}}},
\qquad
E_S \propto \sqrt{\frac{dC_{\mathrm{sca}}}{d\Omega}}.
$$

这里写成“正比”而不是“等于”，是因为省略了与入射场幅值、观测方向和几何归一化相关的比例常数 / it is written as a proportionality rather than an equality because the prefactor related to incident-field amplitude, observation direction, and geometric normalization is omitted.

论文在这里真正需要的是标度关系 / what the paper needs here is the scaling relation:

- 强度量进入干涉问题前要先转成场幅量 / an intensity quantity must be converted into a field-amplitude quantity before entering the interference problem
- 因此 interferometric scattering 会与 pure scattering 的平方根相关 / therefore the interferometric signal follows the square root of pure scattering

这也是 2022 NODI 论文中为什么会说 interferometric scattering signal 与 pure scattering 的平方根相关。

## 3. 干涉增强真正发生在哪里 / Where the Interferometric Enhancement Really Happens

### 3.1 核心公式 / Core Formula

Tsuyama 2022 NODI 论文的核心公式是

$$
I_t
=
|E_D+E_S|^2
=
|E_D|^2 + |E_S|^2 + 2|E_D||E_S|\cos\phi.
$$

也可以写成复数形式

$$
I_t
=
|E_D|^2 + |E_S|^2 + 2\operatorname{Re}(E_D E_S^*).
$$

参数说明 / Parameter meaning：

- $I_t$：探测器记录的总光强 / total detected intensity
- $E_D$：空白通道提供的衍射参考场 / diffracted reference field provided by the blank channel
- $E_S$：颗粒散射场 / particle scattering field
- $\phi$：$E_D$ 与 $E_S$ 之间的相对相位 / relative phase between $E_D$ and $E_S$

### 3.2 为什么参考场能“增强”散射可见性 / Why the Reference Field “Enhances” the Visibility of Scattering

对于弱散射颗粒，通常有

$$
|E_D|^2 \gg |E_S|^2.
$$

于是总信号中最重要的增量项不是 $|E_S|^2$，而是

$$
2\operatorname{Re}(E_D E_S^*).
$$

这意味着：

- 颗粒本身的纯散射可能太弱，单独看不到
- 但只要它与较强的参考场发生干涉，就能通过交叉项把信号抬升出来

因此所谓“干涉增强”，不是让颗粒本身突然散得更强，而是让弱散射通过参考场参与干涉后变得可测。

### 3.3 为什么相位是关键 / Why the Relative Phase Is Critical

干涉项写成

$$
2|E_D||E_S|\cos\phi.
$$

说明增强效果不只取决于振幅，还取决于相位：

- 若 $\cos\phi > 0$，则交叉项为正，信号增强
- 若 $\cos\phi < 0$，则交叉项为负，信号减弱甚至相消
- 若 $\phi$ 随颗粒运动而变化，则脉冲形状和峰值也会变化

因此，Tsuyama 体系真正测到的是“带有相位依赖的干涉脉冲”，而不是一个固定不变的散射常数。

## 4. 为什么小颗粒特别受益于干涉检测 / Why Small Particles Benefit Strongly from Interferometric Detection

在小颗粒极限下，纯散射强度通常更接近

$$
I_{\mathrm{sca}} \propto a^6.
$$

而干涉交叉项更接近

$$
I_{\mathrm{int}} \propto |E_D||E_S| \propto a^3.
$$

参数说明 / Parameter meaning：

- $a$：颗粒半径 / particle radius
- $I_{\mathrm{sca}}$：纯散射强度 / pure scattering intensity
- $I_{\mathrm{int}}$：干涉交叉项贡献 / interferometric cross-term contribution

这意味着：

- 纯散射对小颗粒衰减得非常快
- 干涉检测对小颗粒衰减得慢得多

这正是 NODI 能够测到更小颗粒的重要原因。

$$
\text{channel phase delay}
\to
\text{channel diffraction reference field}
\to

## 0. One Paper-Only Main Chain

If we keep only the scattering/interferometric-enhancement logic described in the Tsuyama papers, the main chain can be compressed into

$$
\text{blank-channel phase delay}
\to
\text{channel diffraction}
\to
E_D
\to
E_S
\to
I_t = |E_D + E_S|^2
\to
\text{pulse-like detectable signal}.
$$

Here

- $E_D$: reference field provided by the blank channel in the diffracted-light region
- $E_S$: particle scattering field
- $I_t$: total detected intensity

Especially important parameters:

- $\lambda$: probe wavelength. It enters the phase delay, the Mie size parameter, and the angular scattering response at the same time.
- $n_s-n_g$: refractive-index mismatch between the solvent and the glass. It determines how strongly the blank channel acts as a phase filter and therefore how the reference field is formed.
- $d$: channel depth. Together with the index mismatch, it determines the optical-path difference and the phase delay.
- $a$: particle radius. It sets the scattering scaling and is crucial for the small-particle advantage of interferometric detection.
- $\phi$: relative phase between the reference field and the scattering field. It determines whether the cross-term is constructive or destructive.

Notation reminder:

- In Section 1, $\theta$ always denotes the phase delay.
- In the Mie-scattering part, the scattering angle is written separately as $\theta_s$ to avoid confusion.

## 1. From Phase Delay to the Reference Field

### 1.1 Starting from the Phase Delay

The starting point in the 2020 diffraction paper is that the refractive index inside the channel differs from that of the glass region, producing an optical-path difference and therefore a phase delay

$$
\theta = \frac{2\pi (n_s-n_g)d}{\lambda}.
$$

Parameter meaning:

- $\theta$: phase delay introduced by the channel
- $n_s$: refractive index of the solvent or medium inside the channel
- $n_g$: refractive index of the glass substrate or wall
- $d$: channel depth
- $\lambda$: probe wavelength

Why the key parameters matter:

- A larger $n_s-n_g$ gives a stronger phase filter and moves the system away from the weak-perturbation limit.
- A larger $d$ increases the optical-path difference, but in the paper logic it enters first through the phase delay rather than as an independent amplitude knob.
- A shorter $\lambda$ produces a larger phase delay for the same depth and index mismatch.

### 1.2 The Channel as a Phase Filter

In the paper picture, the channel can be treated as a finite-width phase window. If the channel width is $W$ and the channel center is at $x_c$, the transverse transmission function can be written as

$$
t(x)
=
1+\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)\Pi\!\left(\frac{x-x_c}{W}\right),
$$

where

- $t(x)$ is the transverse transmission function
- $\Pi(\cdot)$ is the rectangular window function
- $W$ is the channel width
- $x_c$ is the channel-center position

The meaning is:

- outside the channel, the transmission factor is $1$
- inside the channel, the transmission factor is $e^{i\theta}$

### 1.3 How the Post-Channel Field Is Decomposed

If the incident field is $E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)$, then the field immediately after the channel is

$$
E_{\mathrm{after}}(x)
=
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)\,t(x)
=
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)
+
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)\Pi\!\left(\frac{x-x_c}{W}\right).
$$

This is the key step because it splits the field into two parts:

1. $E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)$: the undisturbed transmitted component
2. $E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)\Pi(\cdot)$: the perturbation component generated by the channel-localized phase contrast

So the reference field does not appear out of nowhere. It originates from the phase-perturbation term after propagation.

### 1.4 Propagation to the Back Focal Plane

After propagation to the back focal plane, the field becomes the Fourier transform of the transverse field:

$$
\tilde E(k_x)
=
\mathcal{F}\!\left\{E_{\mathrm{after}}(x)\right\}
=
\tilde E_{\mathrm{in}}(k_x)
+
\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)
\mathcal{F}\!\left\{
E_{\mathrm{in}}(x)\Pi\!\left(\frac{x-x_c}{W}\right)
\right\}.
$$

Parameter meaning:

- $\tilde E(k_x)$: complex field in the back focal plane or angular-spectrum plane
- $k_x$: transverse spatial-frequency coordinate, equivalently an angular-spectrum coordinate
- $\tilde E_{\mathrm{in}}(k_x)$: angular spectrum of the undisturbed transmitted beam

The central point is that the factor

$$
\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)
$$

directly controls the strength of the diffracted component generated by the channel phase contrast.

### 1.5 Intuition in the Weak-Phase Limit

For small phase delay,

$$
e^{i\theta}-1
\approx
i\theta - \frac{\theta^2}{2},
\qquad
|e^{i\theta}-1|
=
2\left|\sin\frac{\theta}{2}\right|
\approx
|\theta|.
$$

This means:

- the diffracted reference-field amplitude is approximately proportional to $|\theta|$
- therefore it is approximately proportional to

$$
\left|\frac{(n_s-n_g)d}{\lambda}\right|
$$

### 1.6 From the Diffracted Angular Spectrum to the Detector-Side Reference Field

The experiment does not collect the entire angular spectrum. It only collects the diffracted-light region. So the detector-side reference field can be written abstractly as

$$
E_D
\propto
\int_{\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{diff}}}
\tilde E_{\mathrm{diff}}(k_x,k_z)\,dA,
$$

where

- $E_D$: channel-diffracted reference field used for interference
- $\mathcal{R}_{\mathrm{diff}}$: diffracted-light region selected by the detector, slit, pinhole, and objective NA
- $\tilde E_{\mathrm{diff}}$: diffracted angular-spectrum component generated by the phase perturbation
- $dA$: area element in the angular-spectrum or back-focal plane

One more crucial point here:

- the transmitted-light region is not used directly as the reference field
- the papers emphasize that the transmitted-light region is spatially suppressed, while the diffracted-light region is retained

So $E_D$ is not “the whole transmitted beam”; it is the **spatially selected channel-diffracted component**.

So the full logic is

$$
\theta
\to
\left(e^{i\theta}-1\right)
\to
\text{phase-perturbation term}
\to
\tilde E_{\mathrm{diff}}
\to
\text{diffracted-light-region selection}
\to
E_D.
$$

This is the real physical meaning of “the blank channel provides the reference field” in the Tsuyama papers.

## 2. How the Particle Scattering Field Enters the Problem

### 2.1 Basic Parameters in Mie Theory

For a spherical particle, define

$$
x=\frac{2\pi n_m a}{\lambda},
\qquad
m=\frac{\tilde n_p}{n_m},
\qquad
k_m=\frac{2\pi n_m}{\lambda}.
$$

Parameter meaning:

- $x$: size parameter
- $n_m$: refractive index of the surrounding medium
- $a$: particle radius
- $\lambda$: probe wavelength
- $m$: relative complex refractive index of the particle
- $\tilde n_p$: complex refractive index of the particle
- $k_m$: wavenumber in the medium

Why the key parameters matter:

- $x$ determines whether the particle is in the Rayleigh regime or a fuller Mie regime.
- The real part of $\tilde n_p$ affects scattering strength and phase, while the imaginary part is related to absorption loss.
- Changing $n_m$ changes both the size parameter and the angular scattering pattern.

### 2.2 Scattering Strength and Angular Distribution

Mie theory gives the total scattering efficiency

$$
Q_{\mathrm{sca}}
=
\frac{2}{x^2}
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}
(2n+1)\left(|a_n|^2+|b_n|^2\right),
$$

the angular amplitude functions

$$
S_1(\theta_s),\qquad S_2(\theta_s),
$$

and therefore the differential scattering cross section

$$
\frac{dC_{\mathrm{sca}}}{d\Omega}
=
\frac{|S_1|^2+|S_2|^2}{2k_m^2}.
$$

Parameter meaning:

- $Q_{\mathrm{sca}}$: dimensionless scattering efficiency
- $a_n,b_n$: Mie coefficients
- $S_1,S_2$: angular scattering amplitude functions
- $C_{\mathrm{sca}}$: scattering cross section
- $\Omega$: solid angle
- $\theta_s$: scattering angle

### 2.3 Why the Paper Moves from Pure Scattering to Field Amplitude

Pure scattering intensity satisfies

$$
I_{\mathrm{sca}} \propto |E_S|^2.
$$

Therefore, if one starts from a pure-scattering intensity quantity, one must convert it into a field-amplitude quantity before entering the interference problem:

$$
E_S \propto \sqrt{I_{\mathrm{sca}}},
\qquad
E_S \propto \sqrt{\frac{dC_{\mathrm{sca}}}{d\Omega}}.
$$

This is written as a proportionality rather than an equality because the prefactor related to the incident-field amplitude, observation direction, and geometric normalization is omitted.

What matters for the paper logic is the scaling relation:

- an intensity quantity must be converted into a field-amplitude quantity before entering the interference problem
- therefore the interferometric signal follows the square root of pure scattering

This is why the 2022 NODI paper states that the interferometric scattering signal is related to the square root of pure scattering.

## 3. Where the Interferometric Enhancement Really Happens

### 3.1 Core Formula

The core equation in the 2022 NODI paper is

$$
I_t
=
|E_D+E_S|^2
=
|E_D|^2 + |E_S|^2 + 2|E_D||E_S|\cos\phi.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
I_t
=
|E_D|^2 + |E_S|^2 + 2\operatorname{Re}(E_D E_S^*).
$$

Parameter meaning:

- $I_t$: total detected intensity
- $E_D$: diffracted reference field provided by the blank channel
- $E_S$: particle scattering field
- $\phi$: relative phase between $E_D$ and $E_S$

### 3.2 Why the Reference Field Enhances the Visibility of Scattering

For weakly scattering particles, typically

$$
|E_D|^2 \gg |E_S|^2.
$$

So the most important incremental term is not $|E_S|^2$ but

$$
2\operatorname{Re}(E_D E_S^*).
$$

This means:

- the particle’s pure scattering may be too weak to observe on its own
- but once it interferes with a stronger reference field, the cross-term lifts it into a detectable range

So “interferometric enhancement” does not mean that the particle suddenly scatters much more strongly by itself. It means that weak scattering becomes visible through the interference cross-term.

### 3.3 Why the Relative Phase Is Critical

The cross-term can be written as

$$
2|E_D||E_S|\cos\phi.
$$

So the enhancement depends not only on amplitude but also on phase:

- if $\cos\phi > 0$, the signal is enhanced
- if $\cos\phi < 0$, the signal is reduced or partially canceled
- if $\phi$ changes during particle motion, the pulse shape and peak value also change

Therefore, what the Tsuyama system actually measures is a phase-dependent interferometric pulse, not a fixed scattering constant.

## 4. Why Small Particles Benefit Strongly from Interferometric Detection

In the small-particle limit, pure scattering intensity is closer to

$$
I_{\mathrm{sca}} \propto a^6,
$$

while the interferometric cross-term is closer to

$$
I_{\mathrm{int}} \propto |E_D||E_S| \propto a^3.
$$

Parameter meaning:

- $a$: particle radius
- $I_{\mathrm{sca}}$: pure scattering intensity
- $I_{\mathrm{int}}$: interferometric cross-term contribution

This means:

- pure scattering decays very rapidly for small particles
- interferometric detection decays much more slowly

This is why NODI can detect smaller particles more effectively than pure scattering methods.

## 5. In the Papers, the Final Target Is the Detectable Pulse

In the Tsuyama papers, the final reported observables are not isolated values of $C_{\mathrm{sca}}$ or $E_S$, but event-level readout quantities such as

- pulse height
- maximum signal value
- count rate
- size-dependent signal trend

So the paper logic can be summarized as

$$
\text{blank channel provides } E_D
\to
\text{particle provides } E_S
\to
I_t = |E_D+E_S|^2
\to
\text{detectable pulse in the diffracted-light region}.
$$

In other words, the papers validate:

- whether the blank channel provides a stable reference field
- whether small-particle scattering can be lifted through the interference cross-term
- whether the lifted signal forms a stable pulse that can be used for particle-size discrimination

## 6. One-Sentence Summary

If we keep only the Tsuyama paper process for scattering and interferometric enhancement, its essence is

$$
\text{channel phase delay}
\to
\text{channel diffraction reference field}
\to
\text{particle scattering field}
\to
\text{interference cross-term}
\to
\text{detectable pulse}.
$$

The two most important physical ideas are:

1. the blank channel first provides the reference field $E_D$
2. small-particle scattering is not mainly observed through the pure scattering term, but through the interference cross-term with $E_D$
